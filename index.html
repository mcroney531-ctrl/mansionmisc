How to Hunt

Each learner will present a slide. Read it carefully under your light.

Click any phrase that you believe is a hallucination — information that sounds credible but may not hold up. Click again to unmark it.

When you're done marking, lock in your answer. But be warned: the mansion will ask if you're sure. And for everything you mark, it will ask the only question that matters:

"How do you know?"

Marking nothing is also an answer — if you believe a slide is clean, lock it in untouched.

The Light Stays On

Two learners. Both used AI. Both mentioned it casually. Only one treated it as the source.

Learner A let a confident answer become a fact — her own experience felt like confirmation, and the AI felt like proof. Two feelings, zero verification. Learner B used AI the way you'd use a knowledgeable colleague: it pointed him somewhere, and he went there himself.

The skill you practiced here was never "spot the lie." Sometimes the slide is clean. The skill is the pause — the willingness to ask "how do you know?" of any claim, any tool, and yourself. Especially when it already tracks with your experience.

Take It With You
The next time something confident lands in front of you — from an AI, a colleague, or your own intuition — give it the half-second pause this mansion gave you. Verification isn't distrust. It's the light you carry into every room.
